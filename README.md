# Introduction to Human Computer Interaction

![Course Cover](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/csinstructiontemplate/emptycourse/refs/heads/main/cover.jpg)

Welcome to the fascinating world where psychology meets pixels! In this course, we bridge the gap between "code that works" and "code that works for people." As a developer, you already know how to build functional websites, but now it’s time to master the art and science of the user experience (UX). We will dive deep into the minds of users, exploring the cognitive frameworks established by industry luminaries like Don Norman and Jakob Nielsen. You’ll learn why certain designs feel intuitive while others lead to frustration, and you'll discover that what we often call "user error" is almost always a design flaw in disguise.

Throughout our journey, we will explore the fundamental laws of interaction design, from the mathematical precision of Fitts’s Law and Hick’s Law to the essential concepts of affordances, signifiers, and mental models. We aren’t just staying in the realm of theory, though. We are taking these principles directly to the browser, applying them to real-world web design decisions. You will learn to craft seamless navigation systems, high-converting forms, and truly responsive layouts that look great on any device. 

We will also place a heavy emphasis on making the web a more inclusive place. By integrating Web Accessibility (A11y) and WCAG standards into your workflow, you’ll ensure your designs are functional for everyone, regardless of their abilities. By the end of this course, you won't just be a developer; you’ll be a user-centric designer capable of identifying common UX mistakes—like "dark patterns" or poor visual hierarchy—and replacing them with industry-standard best practices.

## Course Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

*   **Analyze User Cognition:** Explain how human memory, perception, and mental models influence how people interact with digital interfaces.
*   **Apply Design Laws:** Utilize foundational principles such as Fitts’s Law, Hick’s Law, and Miller’s Law to optimize user interface efficiency and reduce cognitive load.
*   **Implement Best Practices:** Design and build common web components—including navigation menus, input forms, and responsive grids—that adhere to modern usability standards.
*   **Prioritize Accessibility:** Apply Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) to create inclusive digital experiences that accommodate users with diverse needs.
*   **Conduct Usability Evaluations:** Execute rigorous usability testing sessions and use the resulting data to iterate and improve upon design prototypes.
*   **Build a Professional Portfolio:** Complete a comprehensive capstone project that demonstrates a full design-to-development workflow based on HCI principles.

## Course Modules

*   **Foundations of HCI and Human Cognition:** Explore the psychological underpinnings of how humans interact with computers, including mental models, cognitive constraints, and the fundamental laws of interaction design.
*   **Applied Web Design and Interaction Patterns:** Transition from theory to practice by applying HCI principles to common web components, layout structures, and accessibility requirements.
*   **Evaluation Usability Testing and Capstone:** Learn how to measure the success of a design through rigorous evaluation methods and complete a comprehensive design project.
